> “it is better to be a human being dissatisfied than a pig satisfied” — John Stuart Mill.

He's right. We prefer to be humans and suffer because we know stuff that a happy pig doesn't. We are superior then, we can look at the pig condescendingly, sigh and say "I wish I was as happy as this primitive being" then enjoy the feeling of "suffering for a valid reason". Here I argue why I preferred to be the pig if I could. Somewhere else though, [I said why I'm not going to because I don't need it.](2018-11-11_What-is-it-like-to-be-an-existential-nihilist--b65cb4c0007.md) These two stories sound contradicting, aren't they? Not necessarily. Together, I say that personally I also liked to feel happy in my life but I don't *need* to feel happy to be interested in it. It's like, I would like to have pizza for dinner but it's not important enough for me to hate the salad that I'm eating now.

So last night, I was walking back home and after about 10 minutes I noticed that I put myself in a huge dilemma. I was simply imagining hypothetical situations in which I could decide about the global situations and stuff so if I had the power, what would I ultimately wanted for humanity. I followed my confused political ideas about liberty and freedom and equality and all the virtues that we are fighting for, then I asked myself *why are we fighting for them anyway?* what we are hoping to achieve afterwards? What will we have if we build a society in which all of those values are sustainably present? For me, the answer turned out to be the same thing that I usually end up thinking about, HAPPINESS. It’s been years that I keep on reaching this notion of “reward”. I hate it. Seems like everything we do can be traced back to this and I don’t like the way it does. Freud named it as the [pleasure principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pleasure_principle_%28psychology%29) and the harshest critics of psychoanalysis also used the same concept of “maximizing reward” in their [instrumental conditioning paradigms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operant_conditioning): Don’t do what brings the bad feeling and continue doing what gives the good feeling. Even fancy intelligent machines are using the same logic sometimes to learn how to behave: [Repeat the rewarding step](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reinforcement_learning) or [move away from the bad ones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mathematical_optimization) (errors).

![Image](https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1200/0*aCg_ZLY7FxpmKC9t.jpg)

During the past few years I see the shadow of this “fundamental law” everywhere I go and last night I again saw it on my hypothetical utopia. I mean, let’s think about it for a moment. Don’t we want equality because it leads to happiness? turn the question now, isn’t this possible to have freedom but not happiness? in that situation, do we still want it?

I know I’m stepping into some dangerous territories here because I’m throwing concepts that are vaguely defined and [there’s a history of contemplation behind](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosophy_of_happiness) each so just assume we all know what happiness is and we agree on its definition. *I am happy if I feel good*, that’s enough for our purpose here, I hope at least. So I thought we want all of those good things that we advocate now so we all can be happy with our lives, individually and collectively. Going this way then has at least one clear ethical issue, we already can make artificial happiness. Just drop some MDMA into their foods and even the exploited working class is now happy. Problem solved. But set that aside, Imagine we fought for all the values we want right now, freedom and liberty [and so on and so on](http://www.quickmeme.com/img/9f/9f29dc60681fbf2a3184059717b4c41dc3954e08e837e8f27392cd8840da8ea1.jpg), we won and every person in that society can benefit from them. Will this guarantee that they will be happy? I’ll stop making it more complicated here.

**So assuming that these are your only two options, which path you’ll choose as the world’s leader?** I’d go for artificial happiness. It sounds horribly wrong. I know, that’s just wrong in so many levels but I also think it’s a right decision. Here’s why:

You wake up one day, feel refreshed, motivated and optimistic, get out of the bed and look outside the window. The weather is nice, the city is nice, the sky is red and the buildings are ruined but you smile and you see the beauty in it. A spaceship passes from above and you see other’s laughing and cheerfully chatting with one another in it. Your dog barks, time to eat mate, you go outside of your room and see your children giggling and playing, your spouse made breakfast and they embrace you with a glowing face because they are also refreshed and satisfied. You go to the work you love, sitting there taking care of some robots while listening to your colleague’s story about the wild party he went last night. You come back home, no stress, no frustration, chill with your family again and enjoy the evening. You go to bed and close your eyes to have another good sleep.

What is wrong with that? everybody is happy. Literally, everybody feels good in a dystopian city so what is wrong with that? EVERYTHING logically but nothing subjectively. Now reverse the story for yourself, isn’t this possible to wake up feeling kinda like crap checking outside seeing the world is intact and we have all the things we advocated for? Which one do you want to live in personally? I can even take the dystopian city out of your sights giving enough technology tho but nobody really feels bad about it, for them, it’s a beautiful masterpiece that they are looking at. They appreciate it like it’s Jackson Pollock’s artwork, you don’t understand it, they do.

I confess I prefer to feel happy in that dystopian future because we kinda are in the politically correct future of our past generations and it sucks. Imagine telling a homosexual redhead from mediaeval ages that she’s not a witch, she’s loved and allowed to marry whoever she wants today. Now imagine someone from the future comes and tell you that there’s no capitalism, no poverty, no dictatorship and no whatever the hell you want to vanish now. Isn’t that your utopia?

It sounds like I’m trying to convince you to stop fighting for your rights. No. keep on doing that. I’m also doing it as much as I can but what I wanted to tell here is, we might not be happy there because we reached the current milestones. The other thing is, we can already see the artificial happiness coming. Set aside antidepressants, [technology is breaking the barriers,](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Augmented_cognition) A.I and all those buzzwords are just a few years away from us, maybe our grandchildren are the ones who’ll wake up feeling refreshed because our children finally could make [nano-robots that are constantly messing around with the brain circuits](https://www.huffingtonpost.com/entry/ray-kurzweil-nanobots-brain-godlike_us_560555a0e4b0af3706dbe1e2?guccounter=1). Then who needs to seize the means of production anymore?
