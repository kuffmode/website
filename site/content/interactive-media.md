We live in the attention economy, nobody's got the time to read long texts, so digital communication should adapt and move on to more engaging formats. Besides, interaction makes it far easier for people to understand complex concepts. So here we are. This page contains my attempts at creating interactive media to communicate my research and ideas. 

## [Mirage](https://kuffmode.github.io/mirage/)
On the 28th of February 2026, Iranians woke up to a new normal after decades of oppression and struggle. The country was now in an active war with the US and Israel with its supreme leader dead. This war was among a series of events that polarized many Iranians and even others around the world into two camps: those who celebrated the war and those who condemned it. I saw the support within the country as a natural "next step" in a 47-years old game between the state and the people. My thoughts were that, people exhausted every less costly option and started to perceive "asking for external intervetion" as their next least costly option. They found themselves in a situation that getting out of it required bringing down an extensive army that has no limits in suppression. The options were, either I go out and do it myself with some percentage of success and some other percentage of cost (read it _death_) or I can outsource it to _experts_, with a different cost/benefit ratio.

Seeing it this way, I found myself fraustrated by those who thought this reaction was intellectually lazy, un-educated, and people were coerced into wanting it by imperial powers. While, of course, I don't rule out the option, I think it undermines people's agency and their sense of urgency and fraustration. So I decided to vibecode a game where you can make the same decisions as average Iranians made and experience the consequences that followed.
